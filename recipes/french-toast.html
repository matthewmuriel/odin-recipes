French Toast


Description:

French toast is a popular breakfast dish made by dipping slices of bread in a mixture of beaten eggs, milk, and often sugar or vanilla extract, then frying them in a pan until golden brown. It's typically served warm and topped with ingredients like powdered sugar, syrup, fresh fruit, whipped cream, or butter. Some variations may add cinnamon, nutmeg, or other spices to the egg mixture for extra flavor. It's known for its crispy exterior and soft, custard-like interior.

Ingredients

  • 4 slices of day old bread
  • 2 large eggs
  • 1/2 cup (120 ml) milk (any kind, whole milk for richness)
  • 1 tsp vanilla extract (optional)
  • 1/2 tsp ground cinnamon (optional)
  • 1 tbsp sugar (optional)
  • 1 tbsp sugar (optional)
  • Toppings: syrup, powdered sugar, fresh fruit, or whipped cream

Steps:

  1. Prepare the egg mixture: In a shallow bowl, whisk together the eggs, milk, vanilla extract, cinnamon, and sugar until smooth.
  2. Heat the pan: Preheat a large skillet or griddle over medium heat and add a small amount of butter or oil to coat the surface.
  3. Dip the bread: Dip each slice of bread into the egg mixture, making sure both sides are fully coated. Let excess egg drip off before placing the bread in the pan.
  4. Cook the French toast: Place the soaked bread slices in the heated pan and cook for 2-3 minutes on each side, or until both sides are golden brown.
  5. Serve: Remove from the pan and serve immediately with your favorite toppings like syrup, powdered sugar, fresh fruit, or whipped cream.
